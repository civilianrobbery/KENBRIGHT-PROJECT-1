15.1 Module Objective

This module provides a comprehensive overview of insurance finance income or expenses as defined under IFRS 17 paragraphs 87 to 92. The module covers the definition, components, presentation options, and specific exclusions related to insurance finance income or expenses, which represent the financial effects of time value of money and changes in financial assumptions on insurance contract liabilities.

Financial Performance Indicator:

Insurance finance income or expenses represent the financial effects of time value of money and changes in financial assumptions on insurance contract liabilities, providing crucial insights into the financial performance of insurance operations.

15.2 Definition and Components

Insurance finance income or expenses refer to changes in the carrying amount of insurance contract liabilities (or assets) that arise from two primary sources:

Time Value of Money (Discounting)

This represents the accretion of interest on insurance contract liabilities, as future cash flows are discounted to their present value. As time progresses, this discount unwinds, creating finance income or expenses that impact the financial results.

Financial Principle: The time value of money effect reflects the fundamental financial principle that money available today is worth more than the same amount in the future due to its potential earning capacity.
Changes in Financial Assumptions

This includes the impact of movements in key financial variables that affect the measurement of insurance contract liabilities:

  • Interest Rates: Fluctuations in market interest rates directly affect the present value of future cash flows
  • Inflation: Changes in inflation assumptions for inflation-linked benefits or expenses
  • Other Financial Variables: Changes in other market-consistent financial assumptions

15.3 What's Excluded from Insurance Finance Income or Expenses?

Not all changes in financial assumptions are classified as insurance finance income or expenses. Specific exclusions apply in the following scenarios:

Specific Exclusions
Contracts with Direct Participating Features

For contracts where policyholders share directly in the returns of underlying items held by the insurer, certain changes in financial assumptions are excluded from insurance finance income or expenses.

Changes That Would Ordinarily Adjust the CSM

When changes in financial assumptions would normally adjust the Contractual Service Margin (CSM) but specific IFRS 17 rules prevent this adjustment, these changes are instead recognized as insurance service expenses to ensure appropriate matching of revenue and expenses.

Economic Substance Alignment: These exclusions ensure that the accounting treatment aligns with the economic substance of the contracts and prevents potential accounting mismatches.

15.4 How to Present Insurance Finance Income or Expense

Companies have two main presentation options for showing insurance finance income or expenses in their financial statements, with the appropriate choice depending on the type of insurance contract:

A. Non-Participating Contracts (no direct link to underlying assets):
Option 1
Full Recognition in Profit or Loss

Include all insurance finance income or expenses for the period entirely in Profit or Loss (P&L). This approach provides complete transparency but may introduce volatility to the income statement.

Option 2
Split Between P&L and OCI

This approach separates the finance effects between Profit or Loss and Other Comprehensive Income:

  • In Profit or Loss: Show a steady, expected amount based on a fixed ("locked-in") discount rate from when the contract began
  • In Other Comprehensive Income: Recognize the difference between actual changes and the expected amount
B. Participating Contracts (with underlying assets the insurer holds):
Option 1
Full Recognition in Profit or Loss

Include all insurance finance income or expenses for the period entirely in Profit or Loss (P&L). All finance income/expenses are recognized in the income statement.

Option 2
Matching Underlying Items

This approach aligns the recognition of insurance finance income/expenses with the investment returns on underlying assets:

  • In Profit or Loss: Show an amount that matches the investment returns on the underlying items
  • In Other Comprehensive Income: Recognize the remaining financial result

15.5 Foreign Currency Translation

Insurance contracts are treated as monetary items under IAS 21 (The Effects of Changes in Foreign Exchange Rates). This treatment has specific implications:

Currency Treatment:
  • Any exchange differences arising from currency changes are recognized in Profit or Loss
  • The exception applies when exchange differences relate to amounts already recognized in Other Comprehensive Income
  • This ensures consistent treatment of foreign currency effects across financial statements

15.7 Key Implementation Considerations

Discount Rate Determination

The selection of appropriate discount rates is critical for measuring insurance finance income or expenses:

1
Current vs. Locked-in Rates

Decision between using current market rates or rates locked at contract inception

2
Yield Curve Construction

Development of appropriate yield curves for discounting future cash flows

3
Currency Matching

Alignment of discount rates with the currency of insurance contract cash flows

Volatility Management

The choice between presentation options significantly affects income statement volatility:

P&L Volatility
  • Option 1 exposes profit to market rate fluctuations
  • Provides complete transparency of financial effects
  • May result in significant income statement volatility
OCI Volatility
  • Option 2 transfers timing differences to comprehensive income
  • Reduces income statement volatility
  • Provides smoother profit recognition pattern
Risk Management
  • Alignment of accounting policy with risk management strategies
  • Consideration of stakeholder expectations
  • Consistency with overall financial reporting objectives
Disclosure Requirements

Comprehensive disclosures are required to support the chosen presentation approach:

Required Disclosures:
  • Accounting Policy: Clear description of the chosen presentation method
  • Sensitivity Analysis: Impact of changes in key financial assumptions
  • Reconciliation: Movement analysis of insurance contract liabilities
  • Risk Management: Explanation of how finance risk is managed
  • OCI Analysis: Detailed breakdown of items recognized in other comprehensive income
Transparent Reporting: This comprehensive approach to insurance finance income or expenses ensures transparent reporting of the financial effects on insurance contracts, enabling stakeholders to better understand the impact of time value of money and financial assumption changes on an insurer's financial performance.
