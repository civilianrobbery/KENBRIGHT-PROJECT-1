12.1 Introduction

This module provides a detailed overview of the requirements for modifying and derecognizing insurance contracts as per IFRS 17 paragraphs 72-77.

Modification Definition: Modification refers to a change in the contractual terms of an insurance contract after it has been initially recognized. Such changes may result either from a mutual agreement between the insurer and the policyholder or as a result of changes in regulation.

Understanding when and how to account for contract modifications and derecognition is crucial for maintaining compliance with IFRS 17 and ensuring accurate financial reporting.

12.2 When a Contract is Derecognized

According to IFRS 17, an insurance contract must be derecognized and replaced with a new contract only if one or more of the following conditions are met:

a. Distinct Non-insurance Components

The modified contract now contains distinct non-insurance components, such as separate goods or services.

b. PAA Eligibility Change

The contract is no longer eligible to be measured using the Premium Allocation Approach (PAA).

c. Variable Fee Features

The contract either acquires or loses the variable fee features.

d. Pre-existing Modification Terms

The contract included modification terms when the contract was initially issued.

e. Scope Exclusion

The contract no longer falls within the scope of IFRS 17.

f. Contract Boundary Changes

There are substantial changes to the contract boundary.

g. Group Transfer

The contract must be transferred to a different group—for example, a new cohort, portfolio, or due to it becoming onerous.

Important: Changes that do not meet the specified conditions are treated as updates to expected future cash flows and are accounted for in line with IFRS 17 subsequent measurement requirements.

A contract is derecognized if:

  • Any of the above derecognition criteria are met; or
  • The insurer has fully discharged, cancelled, or the obligations to the policyholder have expired

12.4 Accounting Treatment for Derecognition

a) Derecognition of a Contract Within a Group

When a contract is removed from a group (e.g., due to expiry or cancellation), the insurer must:

  • Eliminate the contract's portion of the present value of future cash flows and the associated risk adjustment
  • Recalculate coverage units and revise profit recognized, based on the updated number of contracts in the group
  • Reflect the change in fulfilment cash flows by updating the Contractual Service Margin (CSM), where applicable
b) Derecognition Due to Modification or Transfer

If derecognition results from a modification or a transfer to another entity:

  • The CSM must be updated to reflect the difference between:
    • The change in the carrying amount of the contract, and
    • Either the premium set by a third party (for a transfer), or
    • The premium the insurer would have required if issuing the modified contract as new
  • Account for the modified contract as if this premium had been received on the modification date
IFRS 17 Paragraph 72 Requirements
According to paragraph 72, derecognition and new contract recognition is required when modification:
  • Moves it to a different group
  • Changes its contract boundary significantly
  • Excludes it from the scope of IFRS 17, or
  • Requires different measurement approach

12.5 Detailed Accounting Treatment for Different Scenarios

Scenario 1: Contract Expiry or Cancellation
  • Remove contract from existing group
  • Adjust CSM for remaining contracts
  • Update coverage units calculation
  • Recognize profit/loss for expired contract
Scenario 2: Substantial Modification
  • Derecognize original contract completely
  • Recognize new contract with modified terms
  • Calculate new CSM based on modified terms
  • Reflect any differences in profit or loss
Scenario 3: Transfer Between Groups
  • Remove from original group
  • Add to new group with appropriate CSM allocation
  • Adjust risk adjustment and fulfilment cash flows
  • Update disclosure requirements

12.6 Key Implementation Considerations

Modification Assessment
  • Evaluate whether changes are substantial or administrative
  • Determine if modification criteria in original contract are triggered
  • Assess impact on contract boundaries and measurement approaches
CSM Adjustment Calculations
  • Calculate carrying amount differences accurately
  • Determine appropriate premium benchmarks
  • Allocate CSM adjustments systematically
Group Management
  • Maintain accurate tracking of contract groups
  • Update coverage units promptly
  • Ensure consistent application across similar modifications

12.7 Disclosure Requirements

Entities must disclose comprehensive information about contract modifications and derecognition:

Operational Disclosures
  • Nature and extent of modifications during the period
  • Impact on financial statements of derecognition events
Judgment Disclosures
  • Significant judgments made in assessing modifications
  • Explanation of CSM adjustments resulting from modifications
Reconciliation Disclosures
  • Reconciliation of movements in insurance contract balances
  • Detailed breakdown of modification impacts

12.8 Practical Examples

Example 1: Premium Increase Due to Regulatory Changes
  • If substantial: Derecognize and recognize new contract
  • If minor: Update cash flow estimates in existing contract
  • Assessment based on materiality and impact on contract economics
Example 2: Addition of Investment Component
  • Creates distinct non-insurance component
  • Requires derecognition and new contract recognition
  • Separate accounting for insurance and investment components
Example 3: Change in Coverage Period
  • May affect contract boundaries substantially
  • Could trigger derecognition if significant
  • Assessment required on case-by-case basis

12.9 Critical Success Factors

Documentation
  • Maintain detailed records of all contract modifications
  • Document assessment rationale for derecognition decisions
  • Keep audit trails of CSM adjustments
System Capabilities
  • Track contract modifications and their accounting treatment
  • Automate coverage unit recalculations
  • Support complex CSM adjustment calculations
Internal Controls
  • Establish approval processes for contract modifications
  • Implement review mechanisms for derecognition assessments
  • Ensure consistent application across the organization
