6.1 Introduction

This module provides comprehensive guidance on the subsequent measurement requirements for insurance contracts under IFRS 17, covering paragraphs 40–52. It details the ongoing accounting treatment that entities must apply to update the carrying amount of insurance contracts after initial recognition, ensuring financial statements reflect current estimates and service provision patterns.

Scope: Covers IFRS 17 paragraphs 40-52 on subsequent measurement requirements and ongoing adjustments.

6.2 Core Components of Subsequent Measurement

Liability for Remaining Coverage (LRC)

The LRC represents the insurer's obligation to provide future insurance coverage services. At each reporting date, the LRC is systematically updated to reflect:

Premium Recognition

Inclusion of premiums received during the period

CSM Amortization

Release of Contractual Service Margin to profit or loss as insurance services are provided

Cash Flow Revisions

Changes in fulfilment cash flows relating to future service, including updates to:

  • Expected claim patterns
  • Expense assumptions
  • Policyholder behavior
Risk Adjustment Updates

Revisions to the compensation for non-financial risk uncertainty

Critical Note: CSM adjustments are strictly limited to changes affecting future service periods only.

6.3 Contractual Service Margin (CSM) Mechanics

Permitted Adjustments

The CSM is systematically adjusted each period for:

  • Future Service Cash Flow Changes: Revisions to expected future cash flows that relate to forthcoming coverage periods
  • Interest Accretion: Time value of money impact using the discount rate locked in at initial recognition
  • Service Provision Release: Systematic transfer to profit or loss based on coverage units provided
Prohibited Adjustments

The CSM is explicitly NOT adjusted for:

  • Historical Service Variances: Experience differences related to past or current coverage periods
  • Prior Period Revisions: Changes in estimates that affect services already rendered
CSM Adjustment Illustration
Scenario Parameters:
Opening CSM Balance: BWP 200,000
Period Interest Accretion: BWP 8,000
Future Service Cash Flow Adjustment: BWP -25,000 (unfavorable)
Service Release for Coverage Provided: BWP 30,000
Calculation:
Adjusted CSM = 200,000 + 8,000 - 25,000 - 30,000 = BWP 153,000
Onerous Trigger Condition: If cumulative unfavorable changes in future cash flows exceed the available CSM (including interest accretion), the excess is immediately recognized as a loss in profit or loss.

6.4 Onerous Contract Treatment in Subsequent Periods

A contract group becomes onerous when updated fulfilment cash flows exceed the remaining CSM and liability carrying amount.

Recognition Protocol
CSM Elimination

Immediate reduction of any remaining CSM to zero

Loss Recognition

Excess unfavorable changes recognized immediately in profit or loss

Loss Component Establishment

Creation or augmentation of a loss component within the LRC

Permanence Principle
  • Once classified as onerous, the designation remains fixed throughout the contract lifetime
  • Subsequent favorable changes do not recreate CSM but instead reduce the loss component through profit or loss
  • The loss component mechanism ensures proper tracking of previously recognized losses against future revenue

6.5 Premium Allocation Approach (PAA) Measurement

For eligible contracts, the Liability for Remaining Coverage under PAA is measured as:

Closing LRC = Opening LRC Balance
                + Premiums Received During Period
                - Insurance Acquisition Cash Flows
                + Amortization of Previously Capitalized Acquisition Costs
                + Financing Component Adjustments
                - Insurance Revenue Recognized for Coverage Provided
LRC Adjustment Flow:
Opening LRC Balance → Add: Premiums Received → Less: Acquisition Costs
Add: Acquisition Cost Amortization → Add: Financing Adjustments → Less: Revenue Recognized
→ Closing LRC Balance

6.6 Liability for Incurred Claims (LIC) Composition

The LIC captures obligations for past insured events where claims have been incurred but not yet settled. Periodic updates include:

Core Components
Claims Incurred

New claims arising from past coverage periods

Estimate Revisions

Changes in estimates for:

  • Reported Claims (OCR - Outstanding Claim Reserves)
  • Unreported Claims (IBNR - Incurred But Not Reported)
Discounting Application

Present value adjustment for claims expected to be paid more than 12 months after reporting date

Risk Adjustment Updates

Revisions to non-financial risk compensation

Unallocated Loss Adjustment Expenses (ULAE)
ULAE Treatment
  • Must be estimated using appropriate actuarial techniques
  • Included in fulfilment cash flows for both incurred claims and LRC
  • Subject to discounting when payment timing exceeds 12 months

6.7 Measurement Model Specifics

General Measurement Model (GMM) vs Variable Fee Approach (VFA)
Aspect General Measurement Model (GMM) Variable Fee Approach (VFA)
Foundation Utilizes fulfilment cash flows and CSM Utilizes fulfilment cash flows and CSM
Differentiation Standard approach for most insurance contracts Applies specifically to contracts with direct participation features where CSM adjusts for changes in the insurer's share of underlying items
CSM Treatment Adjusted for future service cash flows and interest accretion Additionally adjusted for changes in the entity's share of underlying items
Comprehensive LRC Adjustment Framework
Opening LRC Balance → Changes from Claims and Expenses
Time Value of Money and Financial Risk Impacts → Risk Adjustment for Non-Financial Risk Revisions
Contractual Service Margin Movements → Loss Component Adjustments (when applicable)
→ Closing LRC Balance

6.8 Key Implementation Principles

Critical Implementation Guidelines:
  • Continuous Updates: LRC and LIC are dynamically updated as new information emerges
  • Profit Timing: CSM ensures profit recognition aligns with insurance service delivery
  • Loss Recognition: Onerous contracts trigger immediate loss recognition without deferral
  • Classification Consistency: Contract profitability classifications, once established, remain fixed despite subsequent estimate changes
  • Comprehensive Expense Capture: ULAE and other claim-related costs must be fully incorporated into fulfilment cash flows using robust estimation techniques
  • Future Service Focus: CSM adjustments are strictly limited to changes affecting future service periods only
  • Permanence Principle: Onerous contract designation is permanent throughout contract lifetime
