14.1 Module Objective

This module provides a comprehensive overview of the components, calculation, and treatment of the insurance service result as specified in IFRS 17 paragraphs 83 to 86. The insurance service result represents the core operational performance of an insurer, excluding investment-related activities, and provides stakeholders with clear insights into the profitability of insurance contract services.

Core Performance Indicator:

The insurance service result represents the core operational performance of an insurer, excluding investment-related activities, and provides stakeholders with clear insights into the profitability of insurance contract services.

14.2 Presentation in the Statement of Financial Performance

As per IFRS 17 standards, insurance companies must disaggregate (split) their financial results into two main categories:

Insurance Service Result

Core operational performance from insurance services

Insurance Finance Income or Expenses

Financial effects from time value and discounting

This fundamental breakdown is presented in the statement of profit or loss and other comprehensive income, also referred to as the statement of financial performance. The separation ensures that users of financial statements can clearly distinguish between the results from insurance services and those from financial activities.

14.3 Reinsurance Contracts Presentation

IFRS 17 mandates that entities present income or expenses from reinsurance contracts held separately from those of insurance contracts issued. This separation enhances transparency and allows for better assessment of the reinsurance program's effectiveness.

Presentation Options:

Entities have two presentation options for reinsurance contracts:

Option 1: Net Presentation

Present a net amount (i.e., the total income or expense from a group of reinsurance contracts held)

Option 2: Detailed Presentation

Present separately the amounts:

  • Recovered from the reinsurer, and
  • An allocation of the premiums paid to the reinsurer
Equivalent Net Effect: Both approaches result in the same net effect but provide different levels of detail to financial statement users.

14.4 Insurance Service Result Components

Insurance Service Revenue

The Insurance Service Result (before reinsurance contracts held) represents the difference between Insurance Service Revenue and Insurance Service Expenses.

Insurance Service Revenue reflects the income earned for providing insurance coverage and other services during the reporting period.

Insurance Service Expenses

Insurance Service Expenses represent all costs related to claims, services, and insurance contract management.

These include incurred claims, acquisition cost amortization, and changes in fulfillment cash flows.

Revenue Composition by Measurement Model:
PAA
Premium Allocation Approach

Insurance revenue typically resembles the earned premium over the coverage period, making it similar to traditional premium recognition patterns.

GMM
General Measurement Model

Insurance service revenue includes four key components:

  • Expected claims and expenses for the coverage provided in the period
  • Risk adjustment for non-financial risk
  • Release of the contractual service margin (CSM)
  • Recovery of insurance acquisition cash flows
Detailed Expense Components:
Insurance Service Expenses Include:
  • Incurred claims and other incurred insurance service expenses
  • Amortization of insurance acquisition cash flows
  • Changes that relate to past service - changes in fulfillment cash flows relating to the liability for incurred claims
  • Changes that relate to future service - losses on onerous groups of contracts and reversals of such losses
Critical Requirement:

A critical requirement under IFRS 17 is that insurance service revenue and insurance service expenses presented in profit or loss must exclude any investment components. This ensures that the insurance service result purely reflects the performance of insurance services rather than investment activities.

14.6 Detailed Component Analysis

Insurance Revenue Recognition

Insurance revenue under IFRS 17 reflects the pattern of service provision rather than simply cash receipt timing.

1
Service Pattern Alignment

Revenue is recognized as insurance coverage and services are provided, ensuring matching with the period of risk coverage.

2
CSM Amortization

The contractual service margin is systematically amortized into revenue over the coverage period, representing the unearned profit recognition.

3
Risk Adjustment Release

The risk adjustment for non-financial risk is released into revenue as the risk expires, reflecting the reduction in uncertainty over time.

Expense Recognition Principles
Incurred Claims

Recognition when the insured event occurs, regardless of when claims are paid.

Acquisition Cost Amortization

Systematic allocation of acquisition costs over the coverage period, aligned with revenue recognition.

Onerous Contract Recognition

Immediate recognition of losses on contracts expected to be unprofitable.

Reinsurance Accounting Treatment
Key Considerations:
  • Separate Measurement: Reinsurance contracts are measured separately from underlying insurance contracts.
  • Presentation Options: Choice between net presentation or detailed disclosure of reinsurance premiums and recoveries.
  • Risk Transfer Assessment: Continuous evaluation of whether reinsurance contracts provide significant risk transfer.

14.7 Implementation Considerations

Measurement Model Selection

The choice between GMM and PAA affects insurance service result calculation:

GMM Application
  • Provides more detailed measurement
  • Requires complex calculations of CSM, risk adjustment, and fulfillment cash flows
  • Suitable for complex, long-duration contracts
PAA Application
  • Simplified approach
  • Suitable for short-duration contracts with coverage periods of one year or less
  • Resembles traditional premium recognition patterns
Transition Adjustments

Implementation of IFRS 17 may require significant adjustments to insurance service result calculation:

1
CSM Establishment

Initial recognition of contractual service margin at transition date

2
Risk Adjustment Calibration

Development of methodology for risk adjustment calculation

3
Acquisition Cost Treatment

Integration of acquisition costs into fulfillment cash flows rather than separate deferral

Disclosure Requirements

Enhanced disclosures supporting the insurance service result include:

Required Disclosures:
  • Revenue Reconciliation: Detailed breakdown of insurance revenue components
  • Expense Analysis: Comprehensive analysis of insurance service expenses
  • Reinsurance Disclosures: Information about reinsurance arrangements and their impact on financial results
  • CSM Movement Analysis: Detailed reconciliation of contractual service margin changes
  • Risk Adjustment Disclosure: Methodology and changes in risk adjustment for non-financial risk
Comprehensive Transformation: This comprehensive approach to insurance service result presentation under IFRS 17 ensures that stakeholders receive transparent, comparable information about an insurer's operational performance, enabling better assessment of business sustainability and profitability.
