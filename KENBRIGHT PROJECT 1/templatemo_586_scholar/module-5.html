5.1 Introduction

This module details the specific requirements for measuring groups of insurance contracts at the point of initial recognition, as mandated by IFRS 17 (Paragraphs 32–52). It establishes the framework for determining the initial carrying amount on the statement of financial position, ensuring it reflects the entity's obligations and rights from the contract.

Scope: Covers IFRS 17 paragraphs 32-52 on initial measurement requirements and model selection.

5.2 Recognition Criteria

A group of insurance contracts must be recognized on the balance sheet at the earliest of the following three dates, ensuring timely capture of risk and economic impact:

1
Beginning of Coverage Period

When the entity is first exposed to insurance risk

2
First Premium Due Date

When a legally enforceable right to receive consideration arises

3
Onerous Identification Date

To prevent delayed recognition of losses

5.3 Measurement Approaches Overview

IFRS 17 provides a suite of measurement models. The applicability of each depends on the specific characteristics of the insurance contract.

Measurement Model Applicability Key Features & Objective
General Measurement Model (GMM) The default model for most insurance contracts, particularly those of longer duration Based on fulfilment value. Components include: Fulfilment Cash Flows (FCF) and a Contractual Service Margin (CSM) to defer profit
Premium Allocation Approach (PAA) A practical expedient for: 1) Contracts with a coverage period of one year or less, or 2) Where the result is not materially different from applying the GMM A simplified model that often resembles existing premium-based accounting. It measures a liability for remaining coverage without explicitly calculating a CSM
Variable Fee Approach (VFA) Specifically for contracts with direct participation features where the policyholder's benefits are heavily linked to underlying items A modification of the GMM where the CSM is adjusted for changes in the underlying items, treating the entity's fee as variable

5.4 Components of Measurement at Initial Recognition

The General Measurement Model (GMM) in Detail

The initial measurement under GMM is the sum of two primary components, ensuring no day-one profit is recognized.

Initial Liability = Fulfilment Cash Flows (FCF) + Contractual Service Margin (CSM)
1. Fulfilment Cash Flows (FCF)

This represents the present value of the entity's net cash flows from fulfilling the contract, encompassing:

  • Future Cash Flows: An unbiased, probability-weighted estimate of all future cash inflows (premiums) and outflows (claims, benefits, direct expenses)
  • Discounting: Adjusts the future cash flows to reflect the time value of money and the financial risks
  • Risk Adjustment (RA): A explicit margin for non-financial risk (e.g., uncertainty in claims frequency or severity)
2. Contractual Service Margin (CSM)

Purpose: The CSM is a liability component that represents the unearned profit of the contract group. Its purpose is to ensure that profit is recognized in profit or loss in proportion to the provision of insurance coverage services over time.

CSM = Present Value of Expected Inflows - (Present Value of Expected Outflows + Risk Adjustment)

The Zero Floor Test: If this calculation results in a negative amount, the CSM is set to zero and the difference is recognized immediately as a loss in profit or loss, identifying the group as onerous.

5.5 Contractual Service Margin (CSM) Calculation & Illustration

Profitable Contract Example

Assume the following for a group of contracts:

Expected Premiums (PV): BWP 3,000
Expected Claims & Expenses (PV): BWP 2,100
Risk Adjustment for Non-Financial Risk: BWP 150
Impact of Discounting (already in PV figures): BWP 250
Calculation:
Fulfilment Cash Flows (FCF) = PV Outflows + Risk Adjustment = BWP 2,100 + BWP 150 = BWP 2,250
CSM = PV Inflows - FCF = BWP 3,000 - BWP 2,250 = BWP 750
Total Initial Liability = FCF + CSM = BWP 2,250 + BWP 750 = BWP 3,000
Interpretation: At initial recognition, the total liability is BWP 3,000. The CSM of BWP 750 will be systematically released to profit or loss over the coverage period.

5.6 Treatment of Onerous Contracts

A group of contracts is onerous when the fulfilment cash flows (the net obligation) are a liability without a corresponding CSM to offset it. This occurs when the present value of expected outflows and the risk adjustment exceeds the present value of expected inflows.

Key Accounting Treatment:
  • No CSM is established (it is set to zero)
  • The entire net loss is recognized immediately in profit or loss
  • The liability is measured at the full amount of the Fulfilment Cash Flows
Onerous Contract Illustration

Assume the following for a different group of contracts:

Expected Premiums (PV): BWP 9,000
Expected Claims & Expenses (PV): BWP 10,000
Risk Adjustment for Non-Financial Risk: BWP 500
Impact of Discounting: BWP (200) [This could be a net finance benefit]
Onerous Loss Calculation:
Fulfilment Cash Flows (FCF) = PV Outflows + Risk Adjustment + Discounting = BWP 10,000 + BWP 500 - BWP 200 = BWP 10,300
Since PV Inflows (BWP 9,000) are less than FCF (BWP 10,300), the contract is onerous
Onerous Loss = FCF - PV Inflows = BWP 10,300 - BWP 9,000 = BWP 1,300
Accounting Impact: This loss of BWP 1,300 is recognized immediately in P&L. The initial liability for the group is the FCF amount of BWP 10,300.

5.7 Premium Allocation Approach (PAA)

The PAA is a simplified model that is often operationally easier to apply than the GMM.

Eligibility (IFRS 17, Para 53)
Eligibility Criteria
  • The coverage period is one year or less, or
  • The entity can demonstrate that the PAA would produce a measurement for the liability for remaining coverage that is not materially different from applying the GMM
Measurement at Initial Recognition
Liability Measurement

The liability for remaining coverage is measured based on the premiums received (or receivable) less any insurance acquisition cash flows paid (or payable).

Acquisition Costs

The entity must choose an accounting policy to either:

  • Defer these costs as part of the liability and amortize them, or
  • Expense them immediately in profit or loss

5.8 Key Takeaways

Critical Learning Points:
  • Recognition is Timely: Driven by the earliest of three key events to ensure transparency
  • GMM is Principle-Based: It measures contracts at current fulfillment value, deferring profit via the CSM
  • CSM is a P&L Deferral Mechanism: It is the unearned profit and cannot be negative. A negative result triggers immediate loss recognition
  • Onerous = Immediate Loss: There is no "negative profit" to defer; losses are recognized upfront
  • PAA is a Simplification: Used for short-duration contracts, it relies on a premium-based measurement
  • Acquisition Cost Treatment Varies: Capitalized and amortized under GMM/VFA, but a choice exists under PAA
