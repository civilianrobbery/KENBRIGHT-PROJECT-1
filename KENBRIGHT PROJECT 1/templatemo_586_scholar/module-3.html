3.1 Introduction

This module provides detailed guidance on how insurance contracts are grouped for measurement and reporting purposes under IFRS 17, covering paragraphs 14 to 24 of the standard.

Scope: Covers IFRS 17 paragraphs 14-24 on contract aggregation and grouping requirements.

3.2 What is Aggregation in IFRS 17?

Under IFRS 17, aggregation refers to the grouping of insurance contracts that share similar risks and are managed together. This structure supports appropriate timing of profit recognition and early identification of losses in financial reporting.

The grouping of these contracts follows a hierarchical process:

1
Establishing Portfolios

Group contracts based on similar risk characteristics and management approach

2
Subdividing Portfolios

Split into profitability groups: onerous, no significant risk, and remaining contracts

3
Creating Annual Cohorts

Further divide into groups of contracts issued within the same calendar year

3.3 Step 1: Establishing Portfolios

Portfolio Allocation Criteria

Contracts must first be allocated into portfolios based on:

Similar Risk Characteristics

Examples include:

  • Product type (life, health, property)
  • Coverage terms and conditions
  • Underlying risk profile
  • Geographical region
Common Management Approach

Key considerations:

  • Contracts managed together as a single pool
  • Consistent underwriting standards
  • Unified risk management strategy
  • Similar operational processes
Consistent Measurement Models

Model alignment:

  • Same accounting model applied
  • GMM, PAA, or VFA consistency
  • Uniform measurement approach
  • Consistent assumption setting
Portfolio Definition: A portfolio consists of contracts that share common features (e.g., health insurance, life products) and are overseen under the same management or operational strategy.

3.4 Step 2: Subdividing Portfolios into Groups

Mandatory Profitability Groups

Once portfolios are defined, each must be further split into at least three distinct groups:

No. Group Description
1 Onerous Contracts at Initial Recognition Contracts expected to generate a loss from inception. These must be identified and measured separately to ensure immediate loss recognition.
2 Contracts with No Significant Risk of Becoming Onerous Contracts expected to remain profitable or at least break even throughout their lifetime. These have minimal risk of future losses.
3 Remaining Contracts Contracts not loss-making initially but may become onerous over time. These require ongoing monitoring and assessment.

3.5 Step 3: Creation of Annual Cohorts

Cohort Formation Rules

After profitability grouping, contracts must be divided into annual cohorts:

Cohort Requirements
  • All contracts issued within the same calendar year form a single cohort
  • Contracts issued more than one year apart cannot be grouped together
  • Cohort composition is fixed at initial recognition and cannot be changed later
  • Each cohort maintains separate measurement and profit recognition
Prohibited Grouping
  • Mixing contracts from different calendar years
  • Changing cohort composition after initial recognition
  • Combining contracts with significantly different issue dates
  • Ignoring the annual cohort requirement

3.7 Use of Premium Allocation Approach (PAA)

PAA Specific Requirements

When applying the PAA, specific considerations apply to contract grouping:

Initial Recognition Assumption
  • Generally assumed contracts are not onerous at initial recognition
  • Evidence required to override this assumption
  • Burden of proof for onerous classification
Ongoing Assessment
  • Regular reassessment of assumptions required
  • Evaluation of changing circumstances
  • Monitoring for potential onerous developments
Important: Insurers must regularly reassess these assumptions and evaluate whether changing circumstances or risks could make contracts onerous over time.

3.8 Contracts Outside the Premium Allocation Approach

Enhanced Assessment Requirements

For contracts not using the PAA, more rigorous assessment is required:

Assessment Area Requirements Implementation Guidance
Onerous Risk Assessment Even contracts not onerous initially must be assessed for risk of becoming onerous Based on possible changes in assumptions that could lead to a loss
Information Sources Comprehensive data analysis required Information derived from the entity's internal reporting systems
Probability Assessment Evaluate significant probability of becoming onerous Based on likelihood of assumption changes and internal reporting data

3.9 Consistency and Grouping Duration

Grouping Permanence Rules

IFRS 17 imposes strict consistency requirements for contract grouping:

Mandatory Requirements
  • Contracts issued more than 12 months apart must not be included in the same group
  • Once contracts are grouped at initial recognition, the composition is fixed
  • Group assignments remain consistent throughout the contract lifetime
  • No changes permitted to group composition after initial recognition
Prohibited Actions
  • Reassessing or changing group composition later
  • Mixing contracts from different annual cohorts
  • Transferring contracts between profitability groups
  • Ignoring the 12-month separation rule
Critical Rule: Group composition is permanently fixed at initial recognition and cannot be reassessed or changed throughout the contract lifetime.
