10.1 Introduction to Reinsurance Contracts Held

This module provides an in-depth overview of Reinsurance Contracts Held as per IFRS 17 paragraphs 60-70. It covers the measurement, presentation, and disclosure requirements for contracts where an entity (the cedant) transfers insurance risk to another entity (the reinsurer).

Module Objective: This module provides an in-depth overview of Reinsurance Contracts Held as per IFRS 17 paragraphs 60-70. It covers the measurement, presentation, and disclosure requirements for contracts where an entity (the cedant) transfers insurance risk to another entity (the reinsurer).
Definition and Purpose

A reinsurance contract held is a contract where one entity (the cedant) obtains coverage from another entity (the reinsurer) for claims arising from insurance contracts the cedant has issued.

Key Changes Under IFRS 17:
  • No more "mirroring approach" from IFRS 4
  • Requires standalone assessment of reinsurance contracts
  • Separate measurement from underlying insurance contracts
Purpose of Accounting for Reinsurance Contracts Held:
Risk Mitigation

Reflect risk mitigation in financial statements

Consistent Measurement

Ensure consistent measurement with underlying contracts

Timely Recognition

Recognize gains/losses on risk transfer at inception

10.2 Measurement of Reinsurance Contracts Held

Initial Recognition

At initial recognition, measure using General Measurement Model (GMM) unless Premium Allocation Approach (PAA) eligibility criteria are met.

Fulfilment Cash Flows
  • Expected present value of future inflows/outflows
  • Adjusted for time value of money
  • Includes reinsurer default risk adjustment
Risk Adjustment
  • Reflects uncertainty in amount/timing of reinsurance recoveries
  • Compensation for bearing reinsurance uncertainty
Contractual Service Margin (CSM)
  • Recognized if PV of reinsurance inflows > ceded premiums
  • Represents unearned profit from reinsurance
  • Amortized over coverage period
Loss-Recovery Component
  • Recognized when underlying contracts are onerous
  • Represents expected recovery of losses from reinsurer

10.5 Presentation in Financial Statements

Statement of Financial Position
Required Separation
  • Reinsurance assets presented separately from insurance contract assets
  • Reinsurance liabilities separate from insurance contract liabilities
  • Clear distinction between insurance and reinsurance activities
Statement of Profit or Loss
Income/Expense Presentation
  • Reinsurance income/expenses separate from insurance revenue/expenses
  • Clear distinction between insurance and reinsurance activities
  • Assets/liabilities from reinsurance vs insurance contracts
  • Income/expenses from reinsurance vs insurance activities

10.6 Disclosure Requirements

Entities must disclose comprehensive information about reinsurance contracts held:

Explanatory Information
  • How reinsurance affects financial statement amounts
  • Nature and extent of risk transfer
Judgment Disclosure
  • Significant judgments in applying IFRS 17 to reinsurance
  • Risk assessment and cash flow estimation methods
Balance Reconciliations
  • Opening/closing balances of reinsurance assets/liabilities
  • Changes from risk adjustment changes
  • CSM recognition/amortization
  • Experience adjustments and discount rate changes
  • Cash flows

10.7 Recognition Criteria

Reinsurance contracts must meet specific criteria for recognition:

Mandatory Criteria
  • Contract provides compensation for insured events
  • Valid contractual arrangement exists
  • Significant insurance risk transfer
  • Reliable measurement possible

10.9 Critical Implementation Points

Measurement Challenges
  • Estimating future reinsurance recoveries
  • Determining risk adjustments for reinsurer credit risk
  • CSM calculation and amortization
  • Coordination with underlying contract measurement
Operational Requirements
  • Robust data systems for reinsurance transactions
  • Cash flow projection modeling capabilities
  • Coordination between underwriting and finance
  • Comprehensive methodology documentation
Important Note: Loss-recovery component recognized when underlying insurance contracts are onerous and related reinsurance contract exists.

10.11 Conclusion: Mastering Reinsurance Contracts Held

Module 10 provides comprehensive guidance on accounting for reinsurance contracts held under IFRS 17. The new standard eliminates the mirroring approach and requires standalone measurement, ensuring proper reflection of risk transfer and financial position.

Key Takeaways:
  • Reinsurance contracts require separate measurement from underlying insurance contracts
  • GMM or PAA approaches apply based on eligibility criteria
  • Four key components: Fulfilment Cash Flows, Risk Adjustment, CSM, and Loss-Recovery
  • Separate presentation in financial statements is mandatory
  • Comprehensive disclosures ensure transparency in risk transfer arrangements
  • Proper implementation requires robust systems and coordinated processes
